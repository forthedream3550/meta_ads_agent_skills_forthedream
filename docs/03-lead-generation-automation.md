# Lead generation automation

## Operating model

Lead generation is treated as a commercial system, not a single cost-per-lead metric.

```text
Relevant reach
-> useful attention
-> lead submission
-> fast follow-up
-> qualification
-> opportunity
-> sale
-> retained value
```

## Required campaign brief

A campaign plan should not be published until the following are known or explicitly marked unknown:

- commercial objective;
- offer and reason to act;
- geography and operational constraints;
- conversion location;
- target customer and exclusions;
- available creative assets;
- daily or lifetime budget;
- start and end conditions;
- primary and guardrail metrics;
- lead follow-up owner and service level;
- CRM or outcome feedback path;
- legal or sector restrictions.

## Automation tasks

### Preparation

- create a naming plan;
- choose a simple campaign structure;
- define conversion and measurement requirements;
- prepare form questions or landing-page requirements;
- define creative hypotheses;
- create a test and decision plan;
- create paused objects before publication where supported.

### Monitoring

- compare spend, reach, frequency and lead volume;
- diagnose changes in response and cost;
- detect missing delivery or tracking;
- compare new, engaged and existing audiences where available;
- compare lead sources with CRM quality outcomes;
- identify follow-up bottlenecks.

### Controlled changes

- pause or activate an object;
- change an ad set budget within policy;
- schedule an approved creative rotation;
- extend or shorten a schedule;
- update approved tracking parameters;
- prepare a replacement object in paused state.

## Decision hierarchy

1. Confirm data integrity.
2. Confirm sufficient observation time and volume.
3. Check sales capacity and follow-up quality.
4. Separate creative, audience, offer and landing-page hypotheses.
5. Prefer one interpretable change at a time.
6. Define the next evaluation point before execution.

## Lead quality

Use outcome stages that the business can reliably provide. A recommended minimum taxonomy is:

- submitted;
- contactable;
- relevant;
- qualified;
- opportunity;
- won;
- invalid or duplicate.

Report both volume and transition rates. Do not optimize only for the easiest stage if it produces low commercial value.

## Fast follow-up

Response speed is part of campaign performance. The agent should flag situations where lead volume rises but first contact or qualification falls. Media changes cannot compensate for an unmanaged sales queue.
