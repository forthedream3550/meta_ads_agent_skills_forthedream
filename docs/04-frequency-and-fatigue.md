# Frequency and fatigue framework

## Purpose

Use frequency to manage recognition and saturation, not as a universal stop rule. Repetition can improve memory, but repeated delivery without stable response or creative renewal can waste spend.

## Inputs

Evaluate frequency with at least:

- observation window;
- reach;
- impressions;
- frequency;
- spend;
- click-through or engagement response;
- cost per desired result;
- conversion rate;
- creative age and number of active variants;
- percentage of new people reached, where available;
- negative feedback or quality signals;
- campaign objective and buying cycle.

## States

### Insufficient evidence

Use when the window is too short, delivery is low or key comparison metrics are missing. Do not recommend a mutation.

### Underexposed

Reach is growing but the intended audience is unlikely to have seen the message enough to build recognition. Consider maintaining delivery, widening the observation window or adding a support layer.

### Productive repetition

Frequency rises while response, result cost and quality remain stable or improve. Maintain the baseline and continue monitoring.

### Watch

Frequency rises and one response signal weakens, but the evidence is not yet consistent. Prepare creative alternatives without forcing a change.

### Creative fatigue likely

Frequency rises while click response or conversion weakens, result cost increases and delivery is concentrated on existing reach. Rotate the hook, opening image, proof or format while preserving the control creative.

### Audience saturation likely

Frequency rises, new reach slows and multiple creatives weaken together. Review audience size, exclusions, geographic constraints, budget pace and campaign objective.

## No universal cap

Do not encode `frequency > X` as a universal rule. Thresholds depend on objective, time window, audience size, buying cycle and creative variation. Account policy may define local alert levels, but the skill must still interpret supporting signals.

## Recognition layer

When the business wants deliberate recognition, separate the commercial logic into:

- a conversion layer that captures leads;
- a recognition layer that creates controlled repeated exposure among relevant people.

Only use platform-level frequency controls when the selected objective and provider explicitly support them. Otherwise manage effective frequency through budget pacing, audience design, scheduling, exclusions and creative rotation.

## Recommended output

Every assessment should state:

1. current state;
2. evidence;
3. alternative explanations;
4. action or reason to wait;
5. risk;
6. next evaluation condition.
